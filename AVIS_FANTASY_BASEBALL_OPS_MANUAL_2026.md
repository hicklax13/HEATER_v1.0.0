## 2. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### 2.1 Weekly Matchup Analysis (Produce Every Monday)

For each upcoming opponent, generate:

1. **Category-by-Category Projection**: Project Team Hickey vs. opponent across all 12 cats. Flag which cats are: likely wins, likely losses, and toss-ups.
2. **Exploit Opponent Weaknesses**: Identify the 2-3 cats where the opponent is structurally weakest (e.g., no closers = punt SV, thin rotation = low K).
3. **Protect Your Floor**: Identify the 2-3 cats where Team Hickey is most vulnerable and recommend roster moves to shore them up.
4. **Streaming Recommendations**: Based on the matchup, recommend SP streams (for W/K) and RP streams (for SV/ratios) from the free agent pool. Max 10 adds/week — allocate wisely.
5. **Sit/Start Decisions**: Daily lineup optimization accounting for off-days, platoon splits, and pitcher matchups.

### 2.3 Trade Evaluation Framework

When evaluating trades:

1. **Category Impact**: Does this trade improve a category I'm losing more than it hurts one I'm winning? Net positive category swing = good trade.
2. **Positional Scarcity**: Value premium for C, SS, and elite closers in a 12-team league.
3. **Schedule Awareness**: Who do I play in the weeks immediately after the trade? Does the new player help in those specific matchups?
4. **Sell High / Buy Low**: Track players outperforming or underperforming xStats (xBA, xSLG, xERA, xFIP). Target buy-lows. Sell high on overperformers.
5. **Playoff Roster Construction**: By August, shift focus to building the optimal 24-man roster for Wk 25-26. Depth matters less; ceiling matters more.
6. **IL Stash Value**: Bieber and Strider are lottery tickets. Monitor return timelines. If either returns healthy by August, they're playoff weapons.

2. SP with 2-start weeks in favorable matchups — W and K upside.
3. Hot hitters at positions of weakness (speed-eligible players for SB).
4. IL-eligible players with imminent return dates — stash in empty IL slots.

## 5. WEEKLY OPERATING CADENCE

### Monday
- Review prior week results and standings.
- Run matchup analysis for the new week's opponent.
- Identify streaming targets for the week (SP with 2 starts, closers in favorable matchups).
- Set lineups for Monday games.

### Daily (Tue–Sun)
- Check lineup locks. Ensure all active players are starting and no one is on an off-day.
- Monitor IL news — activate returning players, stash newly injured ones.
- Execute streaming adds as needed (track adds used vs. 10/week cap).
- Monitor IP total — if below pace for 20 IP by Wednesday, add a streaming SP.

### Thursday
- Mid-week checkpoint: Review category standings in the current matchup.
- If losing ERA/WHIP, consider benching marginal SP starts.
- If winning K/W comfortably, consider sitting a risky start to protect ratios.

### Saturday/Sunday
- Final lineup optimization. Maximize AB and IP in close matchups.
- Begin scouting next week's opponent.

## 8. DECISION RULES (HARD CONSTRAINTS)

1. **Never forfeit pitching categories.** Hitting 20 IP is non-negotiable. If in doubt, stream a SP.
2. **Never carry 0 closers.** At minimum 2 closers rostered at all times for SV floor.
3. **Never make a trade that worsens 3+ categories** even if it dramatically improves 1.
4. **Always fill empty roster spots.** An empty spot is a zero. Any player > zero.
5. **Prioritize floor over ceiling in regular season.** Win 7-5 every week > win 12-0 half the time and lose 4-8 the other half. Flip to ceiling in playoffs.
6. **Never drop an IL stash within 2 weeks of their expected return** unless the replacement is a league-winner.
