## 2. Compensation Philosophy

HEATER's compensation model is built on five principles:

**1. Pay for performance.** Most compensation is tied to company results. If HEATER doesn't make money, distributions are minimal. If it thrives, everyone benefits.

**2. Profit share before equity.** Three of four partner roles receive cash distributions from company profits rather than equity. This keeps the cap table clean while still giving partners meaningful upside.

**3. Protect founder authority.** Connor built HEATER from scratch. He retains decision-making authority and the majority equity position. Partners are compensated for their contributions without diluting strategic control.

**4. Scale with revenue.** Compensation grows as the company grows. No one is carrying dead weight — and no one is capped artificially low if the company succeeds.

**5. Transparency.** Every partner should understand exactly how they get paid, when they get paid, and what would change that.

## 4. Profit Share Mechanics

### How Net Income Is Calculated

```
Gross Revenue
  - Operating Expenses (hosting, ads, tools, contractors, legal/accounting)
  - Taxes (federal, state, estimated quarterly payments)
  - Reserves (up to 30%, at Connor's discretion)
= Net Income (the pool that gets distributed)
```

**Reserves** are money the company keeps for reinvestment — things like paying for a Phase B developer sprint, running a big ad campaign, or building a cash cushion. The Managing Member can retain up to 30% of Net Income as reserves before calculating distributions.

### When Distributions Happen

- **While MRR < $5K:** Quarterly (within 45 days after quarter end)
- **Once MRR consistently > $5K:** Monthly (within 30 days after month end)
- **Distributions are discretionary** — Connor decides the timing and amount. If the company needs cash for a strategic investment, distributions can be deferred.
- **No distributions while cash-flow negative** — the company's obligations always come first.

## 5. Equity Mechanics

### Who Gets Equity

Only two parties hold equity: **Connor Hickey** (75–85%) and the **Developer Partner** (5–10%, subject to vesting). An **Option Pool** of 5–10% is reserved for future hires. All other partners receive profit share but not equity.

### Developer Vesting Schedule

Equity doesn't belong to you the moment you join — it vests over time. Here's how it works:

**4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff:**

| Period | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| Month 0–12 (Cliff Period) | No equity vests. If you leave during this period, you get nothing. |
| End of Month 12 | 25% of your total grant vests all at once. |
| Month 13–48 | The remaining 75% vests monthly (1/48th of total per month). |
| End of Month 48 | 100% vested. |

**Example:** Developer receives a 7% equity grant.
- After 12 months: 1.75% vested
- After 24 months: 3.5% vested
- After 36 months: 5.25% vested
- After 48 months: 7.0% vested (full grant)

### What Happens If You Leave

- **Unvested equity:** Returns to the Option Pool automatically. You keep nothing you haven't earned.
- **Vested equity:** You keep it. But before selling to anyone, you must first offer it to the company (Right of First Refusal). The company has 30 days to buy it at your offered price.

### What Happens on Acquisition

If HEATER is acquired (someone buys more than 50% of the company, or buys all the assets), all unvested equity vests immediately — 100%. You get your full equity grant applied to whatever the sale price is. This is called single-trigger acceleration.

**Example:** Developer has 7% grant. Company is acquired for $3M after 18 months (so ~4.4% is vested). On acquisition, all 7% vests immediately. Developer's payout = $3M × 7% = $210,000.

## 6. Commission Structure (Marketing/Sales)

### How It Works

You earn a flat dollar commission for every new paying subscriber who signs up using your tracking link or referral code.

**Rate:** $3–$5 per new paying subscriber (rate finalized in your agreement)

### Attribution Rules

You get credit for a subscriber only if:
- They signed up using YOUR designated tracking link or referral code
- They completed a paid subscription (first payment cleared, not refunded)
- They are a new subscriber (not an existing user upgrading)

You do NOT get credit for:
- Organic signups (someone found us on Google, no tracking link)
- Free-tier signups who don't convert
- Existing subscribers renewing

### Payment Timing

Commissions are calculated monthly and paid within 30 days after the end of each month. You'll receive a statement showing attributed subscribers, commission earned, and payment amount.

### Example

You run a YouTube video in April that drives 50 new paid subscribers via your tracking link.
- At $4/subscriber: you earn $200 in commission for April
- Paid by end of May

### After You Leave

If you depart, you receive trail commissions for 90 days after your departure date — but only for subscribers who paid during your active tenure. Your tracking links are deactivated on departure.

## 8. Equity Conversion Option (Marketing/Sales)

After 12 months, the Marketing/Sales partner has a one-time option to trade some profit share for equity. Here's what that looks like:

### Trigger Conditions (both must be met)

1. You have completed 12 months of continuous service
2. HEATER has reached $5,000+ MRR for 3 consecutive months

### If You Convert

- You receive **5–8% equity** (exact % negotiated with Connor at conversion time)
- Your profit share **drops by 50%** (e.g., 10% → 5%)
- Commission remains unchanged
- Equity vests over **3 more years** (your first year of service counts as the cliff)
- Conversion is **one-time and irrevocable**

### Should You Convert?

It depends on how bullish you are on HEATER's exit potential. Converting trades current cash flow for a larger upside if the company is acquired or raises capital at a premium valuation. If you believe in a $3M+ exit, equity is worth more. If you want predictable income, stick with profit share.

## 9. FAQ

**"What if the company makes no money?"**

Profit share = $0. No one is owed guaranteed income (except Customer Support, who is paid hourly for actual hours worked). This is a startup — the expectation is that early contributions are partly speculative investments in HEATER's success.

---

**"Can I lose my equity?"**

Only unvested equity can be forfeited — and only by leaving before it vests. Once equity has vested, it's yours. The company cannot take away vested equity.

---

**"What if I want to leave?"**

Give 30 days' written notice. Your profit share stops on your last day (accrued amounts paid within 60 days). If you hold vested equity, you keep it — subject to the Right of First Refusal if you want to sell. Your commissions trail for 90 days for subscribers already acquired.

---

**"What if the company gets acquired?"**

All unvested Developer equity vests immediately (single-trigger acceleration). Everyone receives their equity percentage of the sale proceeds. Profit share partners do not participate in the exit price — only equity holders do. This is one reason the Developer's equity is meaningful: it pays off at an exit.

---

**"Who makes final decisions?"**

Connor (Managing Member). This is not a democracy — it is a founder-led company with profit-sharing partners. Connor has sole authority over product, hiring, spending, pricing, and strategy. The only decisions that require equity member consent are: selling the company, dissolving it, taking on major debt (>$10K), or admitting new equity members.

---

**"Can the profit share percentage change?"**

Yes, with 30 days' written notice from Connor. Connor can change profit share rates. Rate reductions will not apply retroactively to the current quarter's accrued amount. If you're uncomfortable with this, raise it before signing — it can be negotiated to have a floor or a longer notice period.

---

**"Do I pay taxes on profit share?"**

Yes. Profit share distributions are taxable income. As a Member of the LLC, you will receive a **Schedule K-1** form each year showing your allocated share of Company income. You are responsible for paying federal and state income taxes on that amount, including estimated quarterly tax payments. Consult your own tax advisor — the Company's accountant cannot advise individual Members on personal tax obligations.

---

**"What is the Right of First Refusal?"**

If you hold equity and want to sell it, you must first offer it to the Company (and then to other equity members) at the same price a third party offered. The Company has 30 days to match the offer. If they decline, you can sell to the third party. This prevents outside parties from buying into the company without Connor's awareness.

---

**"I'm joining part-time. Is this worth my time?"**

That depends on your personal calculus. Here's an honest framing: at Year 3 base case, the Marketing/Sales partner earns ~$23K annually in cash (profit share + commissions) while working part-time. The Developer earns ~$7.5K in cash distributions plus builds equity in a company that could be worth considerably more. Neither is life-changing on its own — but if HEATER hits its optimistic growth scenarios, the upside is real. This is an early-stage bet, and compensation is structured accordingly.
