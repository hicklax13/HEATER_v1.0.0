## ARTICLE II: DEFINITIONS

As used in this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

**2.1 "Agreement"** means this Operating Agreement, as amended from time to time.

**2.2 "Cliff Period"** means the initial twelve (12)-month period in the Developer's vesting schedule during which no equity vests. Upon completion of the Cliff Period, 25% of the Developer's total equity grant vests at once, and monthly vesting commences thereafter.

**2.3 "Commission"** means the per-subscriber fee payable to the Marketing/Sales Member for directly attributed new paying subscriber acquisitions, as set forth in Article VIII.

**2.4 "Equity Interest"** means a Member's ownership percentage in the Company, which entitles such Member to a share of Net Income distributions (subject to the Profit Share structure in Article V) and a share of the Company's assets upon dissolution per Article XV, proportional to their Equity Interest.

**2.5 "Gross Revenue"** means all revenue received by the Company from any source, including subscription fees, one-time purchases, licensing fees, advertising revenue, and any other income, before any deductions.

**2.6 "Managing Member"** means Connor Hickey, or any successor Managing Member as designated in accordance with this Agreement. The Managing Member has sole authority over day-to-day business operations as set forth in Article XII.

**2.7 "Member"** means any person admitted to the Company as a member, as listed in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.

**2.8 "Net Income"** means Gross Revenue minus: (i) all ordinary and necessary Operating Expenses; (ii) federal, state, and local taxes (including estimated taxes); and (iii) amounts retained as Reserves per Section 5.3. For clarity, Net Income is calculated before profit share distributions but after all business expenses and taxes are paid.

**2.9 "Operating Expenses"** means all reasonable and ordinary expenses incurred in the operation of the Company's business, including but not limited to: server hosting, software licenses, marketing and advertising spend, contractor payments, professional services (legal, accounting), insurance, and any other costs approved by the Managing Member.

**2.10 "Option Pool"** means the equity reserved for future issuances to employees, contractors, or advisors, as described in Section 3.4.

**2.11 "Profit Share"** means the percentage of Net Income payable to a Member as set forth in Article V and Exhibit B. Profit Share is not an equity interest and does not entitle the recipient to ownership of Company assets.

**2.12 "Reserves"** means the portion of Net Income retained by the Company for reinvestment, working capital, and contingencies, as determined by the Managing Member per Section 5.3.

**2.13 "Subscriber"** means a user who has paid for a subscription to HEATER's paid tier and whose first payment has cleared (i.e., not disputed or refunded). Free-tier users do not constitute Subscribers for purposes of Commission calculations.

**2.14 "Vesting Period"** means the four (4)-year period over which the Developer Member's equity grant vests, subject to the Cliff Period and continued service, as described in Article VI.

## ARTICLE V: PROFIT SHARING AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**5.1 Net Income Defined.** For purposes of this Article, "Net Income" means Gross Revenue minus all Operating Expenses, applicable taxes (including estimated quarterly tax payments), and Reserves retained per Section 5.3. Net Income is the pool from which Profit Share distributions are made.

**5.2 Profit Share Allocations.** Subject to Sections 5.3 and 5.6, Net Income shall be distributed as follows:

The Domain Advisor Member must elect either the Profit Share arrangement OR the flat monthly fee set forth in Section 7.2 prior to execution; this election is final for each calendar year and may be changed annually with 30 days' written notice to the Managing Member.

**5.3 Reserves.** The Managing Member may, in their sole discretion, retain up to thirty percent (30%) of Net Income in any given period as Reserves for reinvestment in the business, working capital, payment of future expenses, or contingencies. Reserves shall be held in the Company's operating account and shall not be distributed without Managing Member approval.

**5.4 Distribution Schedule.**

(a) While Company monthly revenue is below $5,000: distributions shall be made quarterly (within 45 days after the end of each calendar quarter), at the Managing Member's discretion.

(b) Once Company monthly revenue consistently exceeds $5,000 for three (3) consecutive months: distributions may be made monthly (within 30 days after the end of each calendar month), at the Managing Member's discretion.

(c) All distributions are discretionary. The Managing Member is not required to distribute any particular amount and may defer or reduce distributions for legitimate business reasons.

**5.5 Priority.** Distributions shall be made in the following order:

1. Payment of all current Operating Expenses and liabilities;
2. Retention of Reserves (up to 30% of Net Income);
3. Profit Share distributions to Members in the percentages set forth in Section 5.2;
4. Remainder to Connor Hickey as Managing Member.

**5.6 No Distributions While Cash-Flow Negative.** No distributions of any kind shall be made to any Member if the Company is cash-flow negative (i.e., current liabilities exceed current liquid assets, or making the distribution would cause the Company to be unable to meet its obligations as they come due).

**5.7 Tax Distributions.** The Managing Member may, in their discretion, cause the Company to make tax distributions to Members to assist with estimated tax obligations arising from Company income allocated to such Members. Any such tax distributions shall be credited against future Profit Share distributions.

**5.8 No Return of Distributions.** Except as required by applicable law, no Member shall be required to return any distribution received from the Company.

## ARTICLE VI: EQUITY VESTING (DEVELOPER ROLE)

**6.1 Equity Grant.** The Developer Member is granted an equity interest of [5%–10%] (to be specified in Exhibit A) subject to the vesting schedule set forth in this Article. Until vested, unvested equity is counted in the Option Pool and does not entitle the Developer Member to any distributions or ownership rights.

**6.2 Vesting Schedule.**

(a) **Cliff:** No equity vests during the first twelve (12) months of continuous service (the "Cliff Period"). If the Developer Member's service ends for any reason before the Cliff Period is complete, all equity is forfeited and returns to the Option Pool.

(b) **Post-Cliff Vesting:** Upon completion of the Cliff Period, twenty-five percent (25%) of the total grant vests immediately. Thereafter, the remaining seventy-five percent (75%) vests in equal monthly installments of 1/48th of the total grant per month, measured from the Developer Member's start date, until the full grant is vested at the end of the four (4)-year Vesting Period.

(c) **Service Requirement:** Vesting requires continuous service to the Company. Continuous service means the Developer Member is actively performing their role and has not given or received notice of termination.

**6.3 No Accelerated Vesting.** Except as provided in Section 6.6 (Change of Control), there is no accelerated vesting. Standard departure for any reason does not accelerate vesting.

**6.4 Termination of Service.**

(a) **Unvested Equity:** All unvested equity as of the date of termination (whether voluntary or involuntary, with or without cause) automatically and immediately returns to the Option Pool. No compensation is owed for unvested equity.

(b) **Vested Equity:** The Developer Member retains all vested equity subject to the Company's Right of First Refusal set forth in Section 6.5.

(c) **Profit Share:** The Developer Member's Profit Share terminates immediately upon termination of service. No Profit Share is owed for any period after the date of termination, except for amounts accrued but not yet distributed as of the termination date, which shall be paid within 60 days.

**6.5 Right of First Refusal.** Before selling, transferring, or otherwise disposing of any vested equity, the Developer Member must first offer the Company (and then, if the Company declines, the other equity Members) the right to purchase such equity at the same price and on the same terms as the proposed transfer. The Company shall have thirty (30) days to exercise the Right of First Refusal. Any purported transfer in violation of this Section is void.

**6.6 Change of Control Acceleration.** Upon a Change of Control (defined as: an acquisition of the Company by a third party in which more than 50% of the Company's equity is transferred, or a sale of all or substantially all of the Company's assets), all unvested equity held by the Developer Member shall immediately and automatically vest in full (single-trigger acceleration). This acceleration provision applies only to the Developer Member and only upon a bona fide Change of Control.

**6.7 409A Considerations.** The parties acknowledge that this Agreement may have tax implications, including potential application of Section 409A of the Internal Revenue Code. The Company's attorney and/or accountant should review the vesting and equity grant provisions for tax compliance prior to execution.

## ARTICLE VIII: COMMISSION STRUCTURE (MARKETING/SALES)

**8.1 Commission Rate.** The Marketing/Sales Member shall earn a commission of $3.00–$5.00 per new paying Subscriber (exact rate set forth in Exhibit B) directly attributed to the Marketing/Sales Member through a valid referral or tracking link or code.

**8.2 Attribution Requirements.** Commission is earned only on:

(a) New Subscribers (not renewals, upgrades, or existing users);

(b) Subscribers who signed up using the Marketing/Sales Member's designated tracking link, referral code, or other attribution mechanism maintained by the Company;

(c) Subscribers whose first payment has cleared and is not subsequently disputed or refunded within 30 days.

**8.3 No Commission on Organic Conversions.** No commission is payable on Subscribers acquired through organic search, direct navigation, word-of-mouth not tied to a tracking link, or any other channel not directly attributed to the Marketing/Sales Member's promotional activities.

**8.4 Payment Timing.** Commissions are calculated monthly and paid within 30 days after the end of each calendar month in which the attributed Subscriber's first payment clears. Commission statements shall be provided to the Marketing/Sales Member monthly.

**8.5 No Commission on Free Tier.** Users who sign up for a free tier (if any) and do not convert to a paid subscription are not Subscribers and do not generate Commission.

**8.6 Annual Review.** Commission rates shall be reviewed annually by the Managing Member. The Managing Member may adjust commission rates with 30 days' written notice to the Marketing/Sales Member. Rate reductions shall not apply to Subscribers already attributed prior to the effective date of the rate change.

**8.7 No Minimum Commission Guarantee.** No minimum commission amount is guaranteed. If no Subscribers are attributed to the Marketing/Sales Member in a given month, no commission is payable for that month.

## ARTICLE IX: EQUITY CONVERSION OPTION (MARKETING/SALES)

**9.1 Conversion Trigger.** After twelve (12) months of continuous service to the Company, the Marketing/Sales Member shall have a one-time option to convert their Profit Share arrangement to an equity interest in the Company, provided that both of the following conditions are met at the time of conversion:

(a) The Marketing/Sales Member has provided continuous, active service for at least 12 consecutive months; AND

(b) The Company has achieved monthly recurring revenue (MRR) of at least $5,000 for three (3) consecutive months immediately preceding the conversion request.

**9.2 Conversion Terms.** If the conditions in Section 9.1 are met and the Marketing/Sales Member elects to convert:

(a) The Marketing/Sales Member shall receive an equity interest of 5%–8% (exact percentage subject to Managing Member approval at time of conversion, based on Company performance and contributions);

(b) The equity shall vest over the remaining three (3) years of a standard four-year vesting period (with the first year of service credited toward the cliff), subject to the terms of Article VI applied mutatis mutandis;

(c) The Marketing/Sales Member's Profit Share percentage shall be reduced by fifty percent (50%) (e.g., if Profit Share was 10%, it reduces to 5%) from the effective date of conversion;

(d) Commission under Article VIII continues unchanged regardless of conversion.

**9.3 One-Time Election.** The conversion option is a one-time right. Once exercised, it cannot be reversed. If the Marketing/Sales Member elects not to convert at the 12-month mark, the option expires unless the Managing Member agrees in writing to extend it.

**9.4 Managing Member Approval.** Conversion is subject to the Managing Member's approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if the conditions in Section 9.1 are objectively met.

**9.5 Documentation.** Upon conversion, the parties shall execute an amended Exhibit A reflecting the new equity structure and any other documentation required by the Managing Member or the Company's attorney.

## ARTICLE X: TERMINATION AND DEPARTURE

**10.1 Voluntary Departure.** Any Member may voluntarily depart the Company by providing thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the Managing Member. The Member shall reasonably cooperate during the notice period to ensure an orderly transition.

**10.2 Termination by Managing Member.** The Managing Member may terminate any Member's service relationship at any time, with or without cause, effective immediately upon written notice. The Managing Member is not required to provide a reason for termination.

**10.3 Effect on Profit Share.** A departing Member's Profit Share terminates effective on the departure date (or such earlier date as may be agreed). The Company shall pay any accrued but unpaid Profit Share within sixty (60) days of the departure date, pro-rated through the last day of service.

**10.4 Effect on Equity.**

(a) All unvested equity as of the departure date automatically returns to the Option Pool.

(b) All vested equity is retained by the departing Member, subject to the Right of First Refusal in Section 6.5 and the transfer restrictions in Section 3.5.

**10.5 Effect on Commissions.** The Marketing/Sales Member shall receive trail commissions for 90 days following the departure date, but only for Subscribers acquired (i.e., first payment cleared) during the Member's tenure. No commissions are payable for Subscribers acquired after the departure date, even if the Subscriber used a tracking link previously attributed to the departing Member (tracking links shall be deactivated upon departure).

**10.6 Non-Compete.** For a period of twelve (12) months following the departure date, each departing Member (other than Customer Support, for whom no non-compete applies) agrees not to directly work for, consult with, invest in (other than passive investments of less than 2%), or found a competing business that offers fantasy baseball or fantasy football analytics software products that are substantially similar to HEATER's core product offering. This non-compete is limited in scope to direct competition with HEATER's products; it does not prevent participation in the fantasy sports industry generally.

**10.7 Non-Solicitation.** For a period of twenty-four (24) months following the departure date, each departing Member agrees not to:

(a) Solicit or attempt to solicit any HEATER Subscriber or user to use a competing product; or

(b) Solicit, recruit, or encourage any other Member or HEATER employee or contractor to terminate their relationship with the Company.

**10.8 Pennsylvania Law on Non-Competes.** The parties acknowledge that Pennsylvania courts scrutinize non-compete agreements and may modify or refuse to enforce overly broad restrictions. The non-compete in Section 10.6 is intended to be narrowly tailored to protect legitimate business interests. If any portion is found unenforceable, the remainder shall be enforced to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## ARTICLE XIV: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**14.1 Good Faith Negotiation.** Before initiating formal dispute resolution, the disputing parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation for a period of not less than fifteen (15) days.

**14.2 Mediation.** If good-faith negotiation fails, the parties shall submit the dispute to non-binding mediation under the rules of the American Arbitration Association (AAA), conducted in [County], Pennsylvania. The cost of the mediator shall be split equally. Mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of initiation unless otherwise agreed.

**14.3 Binding Arbitration.** If mediation fails to resolve the dispute, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the AAA, with a single arbitrator, conducted in [County], Pennsylvania. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

**14.4 Attorney Fees.** In any arbitration or litigation to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover from the non-prevailing party all reasonable attorney fees, arbitrator fees, and costs.

**14.5 Injunctive Relief.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, any party may seek emergency injunctive or other equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm (e.g., to enforce the IP assignment or confidentiality provisions) without first pursuing mediation or arbitration.
