# Combustion Index

### A Visual Philosophy

---

**Combustion Index is the discipline of measuring fire.** It holds two temperatures
at once — the white-hot violence of ignition and the cold, exacting calm of the
instrument that records it. Every composition is a controlled burn: raw energy
seized and held inside a rigorous analytical frame, so that something fleeting and
furious can be studied, mapped, and trusted. The aesthetic lives in that tension
between heat and measurement, between the blur of velocity and the stillness of the
gauge that captures it. Nothing is decorative. Every mark is a reading.

**Space and form.** The field is a deep charcoal-navy void — the dark of a stadium
at night, the black behind a filament. Forms do not sit *on* this field; they
*emerge* from it, lit from within. Negative space is not emptiness but a cooling
chamber: generous, deliberate, giving each hot element room to radiate without
scorching its neighbor. An invisible grid governs everything — the laboratory bench,
the strike zone, the armature beneath the burn. Alignment is absolute; the eye should
feel the order even where it cannot name it. This precision is the product of
painstaking attention, the kind that only reads as effortless because it was labored
over for hours.

**Color and material.** There is one true light source: a single saturated orange,
the color of metal at the moment it yields. It appears against deep navy and bone-white
only where heat genuinely lives — the live element, the urgent number, the action. Used
sparingly, it carries enormous weight; used freely, it would lose all meaning, so it is
rationed with a master's restraint. A narrow ember-to-flame ramp supplies gradient and
glow; cooler greys and a clean off-white carry everything that is merely *recorded*
rather than *burning*. Surfaces are honest and matte, like instrument housings and
pressed paper, so the orange reads as emitted light rather than printed ink.

**Scale and rhythm.** Type behaves like apparatus. Display lettering arrives as
sculptural mass — broad, confident, structural — set beside whisper-quiet labels and
tabular figures that read like a calibrated readout. The drama is in the interval: one
monumental numeral standing in a constellation of tiny annotations; a single bold word
anchoring a field of fine ticks. Repetition builds meaning the way a scientific plate
earns trust — measurement scales, tick marks, the patient seam-arcs of a sphere in
motion, accumulating until the page rewards sustained looking. Rhythm is metronomic
where it counts and silent everywhere else.

**Composition and hierarchy.** Information is presented as evidence. Each layout is a
plate from an imaginary field manual — annotated, indexed, reverent toward the thing it
documents. Balance is asymmetric and earned: a heavy hot mass on one side answered by a
quiet expanse of fine marks on the other, the whole held in tension rather than
symmetry. Hierarchy is thermal — the hottest element is the most important, and the eye
travels by temperature. Borders are few; separation comes from space, weight, and a
single decisive line rather than boxes within boxes.

**Craftsmanship is the whole point.** This work must look meticulously crafted — the
product of deep expertise, executed at the absolute top of the field. Every kerned
label, every gradient stop, every interval of negative space should feel deliberate and
re-refined, as though a master returned to it again and again until nothing could be
removed. The finish is crisp to the pixel; nothing is approximate, nothing is
accidental. The result should feel less *designed* than *instrumented* — calm, exact,
and quietly on fire.
