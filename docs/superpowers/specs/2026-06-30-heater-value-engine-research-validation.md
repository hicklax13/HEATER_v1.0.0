**Prepared for:** the coordinator (for presentation to the product owner, Connor)

## (B) THE FINAL VALIDATED DESIGN

The engine is a **single source of truth** for every surface, built in tiers over one shared stochastic player model. All seven corrections (heteroscedastic shrinkage, asymmetric Statcast, NB/Skellam, control-variate, OCBA, hierarchical parameter draw, policy completeness) are folded in.

**Layer 0 — Shared player model (one per player, feeds every surface):**
- **Mean projection:** ridge-stacked 7 systems, weights **per-category + double-shrunk** (toward equal-weights *and* toward zero), with a **hard equal-weight OOS fallback**; Marcel reliability-regression + aging as transparent (non-learned) layers; regress toward the *projection prior*, not raw league mean.
- **Skill regression (asymmetric):** hitters → capped barrel%/EV-air/xwOBA nudges; pitchers → SIERA/xFIP anchor with **large variance**, K via CSW%/K-BB%, **no pitcher xERA overrides**, **de-confounded/down-weighted Stuff+**. Recency = low-weight update to fast-stabilizing skill inputs only.
- **Per-player posterior (distribution, not point):** **heteroscedastic** true-talent posterior per category via **SURE/variance-dependent shrinkage** over variance-stabilized rates (sample-size-proportional), supplying **σ² (player-to-player) and τ² (week-to-week)** separately. **NB** for counting cats, **beta-binomial** for rate cats — *unequal variance across players* (drops G-score's equal-variance assumption).
- **Availability survival distribution (NEW, G1):** per-player games/IP-remaining + current-injury hazard.

**Layer 1 — Cheap analytic proxy (instant grades, ranks the wide candidate set):**
- Per-category **P(win/tie/loss)** via **analytic Skellam** (counting, **explicit tie mass**) + Welch/Normal on volume-weighted ratio (rate cats) — Rao-Blackwellized (lower variance than draw-counting).
- Threshold-aware **Δ category-wins → Δ playoff-odds** ladder.
- **G-score (display only):** interpretable per-player scalar surfaced in UI/ranking; **never** the probability engine.
- **Same marginal + dependence model as Layer 2** (mandatory — see Risk 2).

**Layer 2 — Deep policy-aware paired-MC (finalists only, async):**
- **Hierarchical two-level sampling (NEW, G3):** per replicate, draw player true-talent rates from posteriors *then* draw weekly outcomes; sample availability from survival curves.
- **Cross-category dependence:** **Gaussian copula** (Cholesky→Φ→inverse-NB/beta-binomial), latent correlation **shrunk (Ledoit-Wolf) and calibrated jointly with the margins via rank-correlation matching** (NEW, G4).
- **Policy-aware play of ALL 12 teams (NEW, G6+G7):** each simulated week, re-solve the weekly LP **optimizing Δ P(win matchup)** (not Δ E[points]) **and** run a **bounded streaming/FA replacement step** within the 10-add budget — for the user *and* opponents.
- **Season → top-4 → bracket** simulated (Bradley-Terry/log5 matchups); grade = **Δ E[placement utility]** ≡ benchmark-relative Δ-win-prob up the ladder.
- **Variance reduction stack:** paired **CRN** (synchronize streams per-player/per-event; perturb only swapped players) ∘ **antithetic** ∘ **proxy as control variate**.
- **Budget across candidates (NEW, G2):** **OCBA** allocation + sequential stopping (stop a trade's sims once its band separates from the leader or crosses zero).

**Grading & honesty:**
- **Shrinkage ladder** (Δcat-wins → Δplayoff → Δchamp), blend weight chosen by **CPCV** OOS proper-score, **deflated** (DSR/PBO) with a **MinBTL** power check, shrunk toward equal-weight.
- Report **uncertainty band** from paired differences; **"no detectable edge"** when it straddles zero (indifference zone).
- Probabilistic outputs ranked by **Brier/log-loss**; intervals via **Adaptive/weighted Conformal**; variants certified for the single-source-of-truth role via **DM + Model Confidence Set**.

**Finder vs Evaluator:**
- **Evaluator** (user brings a trade): one-sided, your Δ-value only.
- **Finder:** P(accept) × max(0, your Δ-value) + mutual-benefit filter, where **opponent Δ-value is computed** (same engine on their roster — superset of ANAC preference models given full observability) and **P(accept) is a calibrated, league-history-updated classifier** (primary feature = opponent surplus; +eBay-proven context features incl. **apparent/headline fairness on raw player value**, manager activity, contender status, roster-hole urgency, #pieces; Platt→isotonic calibration; Bayesian cold-start prior). Present fairness as a **range**, acceptance as a **probability with a band** (Myerson-Satterthwaite: no objective fair price exists).

## (C) PRIORITIZED UPGRADE LIST (vs the pre-research synthesis), by impact

**Tier 1 — Correctness-critical (the design is *wrong* without these):**
1. **Resolve the G-score ↔ NB/Skellam contradiction (G5).** The two flagship recommendations are mutually inconsistent. G-score = display scalar; probabilities from heteroscedastic NB/Poisson. *This is the single most dangerous architectural inconsistency — a proxy whose distributional model disagrees with the finalist sim silently mis-ranks the candidate set the deep tier never re-examines.*
2. **Unify the marginal + dependence model across BOTH tiers.** Same NB/Skellam margins + same shrunk discrete copula in proxy and deep MC. Periodically deep-MC a random sample of proxy-*rejected* trades to measure rank agreement (Spearman) + recall.
3. **Hierarchical parameter-uncertainty propagation (G3).** Draw true-talent from posteriors *then* outcomes. Without it, every champ/playoff band is falsely tight and the honesty mechanism fails (verified: Demsyn-Jones).
4. **Policy completeness (G6+G7): simulate opponents policy-aware + include the weekly streaming step.** Frozen opponents / lineup-only policy bias **every** grade toward "your trade helps" and mis-value pitching/streaming trades.
5. **Calibrate the variance terms + correlation matrix against the backtest (Risk 1).** Hand-set CVs (`scenario_generator.py` DEFAULT_CV "~50%" guesses) must be replaced or the engine precisely estimates a *biased* number — no variance reduction can fix that.

**Tier 2 — High-impact accuracy/efficiency:**
6. **Heteroscedastic SURE shrinkage** over variance-stabilized rates (Subject 2a). Plugs into existing Kalman-variance + backtest loop; TSE ≈0.42 vs ≈0.59.
7. **Asymmetric per-category Statcast layer** (Subject 3): no pitcher xERA overrides, SIERA/xFIP + large pitcher variance, de-confound Stuff+. *(Verify locally it doesn't double-apply with existing `fa_recommender`/sustainability.)*
8. **NB margins + explicit Skellam tie mass + beta-binomial rates** (Subject 4).
9. **Availability survival layer (G1)** — largest unmodeled real value swing.
10. **OCBA + control-variate + sequential stopping (G2 + Subject 5)** — makes the policy-aware MC tractable inside latency budget (~1e3–5e3 paired seasons/finalist).
11. **Demote raw recency** to a skill-input update only (Subject 2b) — stops the engine chasing noise across every surface.

**Tier 3 — Rigor / validation machinery (cheap to add, prevents overfitting & false confidence):**
12. **CPCV + Deflated Sharpe/PBO + MinBTL** for the shrinkage-weight selection (Subject 8a/b) — else "backtest-tuned" = "backtest-overfit" on a near-unpowered 1-bit champ label.
13. **Discrete-copula joint calibration via rank-correlation matching (G4).**
14. **DM + Model Confidence Set** to certify the single-source-of-truth variant; **Adaptive Conformal** intervals; rank by **Brier/log-loss** (Subject 8c/d).
15. **Calibrated, league-history-updated P(accept) classifier** with apparent-fairness feature (Subject 7) — the Finder's only non-SOTA piece.

**Tier 4 — Optional, when compute allows:**
16. Per-category double-shrunk stacking weights + equal-weight fallback (Subject 1).
17. Multi-week stochastic lookahead + backtest-fitted terminal value function (Subject 7).

## (D) HONEST VERDICT

**Is this now provably SOTA-or-better?**

- **Architecture: yes, with high confidence.** Every published method I checked is a strict special case or a deliberately-degraded approximation of this design — Rosenof's normal-only objective (cannot represent ties or the bracket), RotoGraphs' roto-only sim, the GA fantasy-trade optimizer's static-points objective, baseballforecaster's single-objective MC, the SABR season-level Pythagorean model (no category structure). No source does the four win-prob sub-problems correctly *together*, and **no fantasy tool grades trades by a policy-aware Δ-championship simulation.** The objective is the canonical one (Haugh-Singal, verified) and the method class is the textbook one (Bertsekas rollout). The corrected design would be a genuine, defensibly-publishable first.

- **"Provably SOTA" is the wrong bar for the *numbers*, and the design correctly admits this.** For a marginal trade, the true Δ-champ (~0.5–2%) is genuinely below the combined schedule-luck + projection-error noise floor (G3 + G6). No estimator can manufacture signal that isn't there. The honest target is **calibrated, decision-useful, and self-aware about its own uncertainty** — which the shrinkage ladder + uncertainty band + "no detectable edge" delivers, *but only after the Tier-1 corrections* (most importantly G3 — without hierarchical parameter draws the bands are dishonestly narrow).

**Where residual uncertainty remains, and what would close it:**

1. **Variance/correlation calibration is unverified empirically.** The whole edge rests on per-category τ²/dispersion-r and the cross-category ρ matrix, and HEATER's current defaults are hand-set guesses. *Close it:* fit them on the historical backtest; validate by **reliability/coverage on realized weekly category outcomes** (the design has the framework). This is the highest-value open item.
2. **Availability modeling has no academic prior (G1).** It's a true frontier gap, so it must be **built and validated in-house** (calibrate the hazard against your own injury logs); expect this to be the noisiest component initially.
3. **Proxy↔deep rank-agreement is an assumption until measured.** *Close it:* the recall audit in upgrade #2 (deep-MC a sample of proxy-rejected trades).
4. **Compute/latency at the candidate-set scale is unproven.** Policy-aware re-solve × 26 weeks × playoffs × hierarchical draws × OCBA across candidates is heavy. *Close it:* prototype the finalist tier and measure wall-time before wiring it behind interactive surfaces; keep interactive surfaces on the cheap tier, deep tier async.
5. **The 1-bit champ label is near-unpowered** — even with CPCV+DSR, you may simply lack the data to *prove* a blend-weight choice beats equal-weight. *Close it:* lean on the surrogate ladder (evaluate on Δcat-wins/Δplayoff, the higher-information rungs) and shrink toward equal-weight when the deflated test is inconclusive.

**One caution against overclaiming:** "single source of truth for every surface" is correct strategy but is also a coupling risk (Risk 8) — a miscalibration or a slow path propagates app-wide. Gate every surface behind the cheap tier interactively, run the deep tier async, and require the **DM/MCS certification** before any variant becomes canonical.

### Gap 3

The panel's variance-reduction subject implicitly assumes the projection MEANS are fixed and you only sample game/week OUTCOMES. FIX: each MC season must FIRST draw each player's true-talent rate from its posterior (the Bayesian/KDE/Kalman layer HEATER already builds), THEN draw outcomes — a hierarchical two-level sample — so the champ/playoff bands widen honestly. ### Gap 4
DISCRETE-MARGIN COPULA IDENTIFIABILITY CAVEAT (a real flaw in subject #4's recommendation). Subject #4 recommends 'Gaussian copula (Cholesky→Φ→inverse) with NB/Skellam margins.' But Sklar's theorem loses its margin-free property when margins are DISCRETE: the copula is NOT unique, and the fitted latent correlation is entangled with the marginal parameters — so you cannot separately estimate 'the correlation matrix' and 'the marginals' and bolt them together; the dependence you induce depends on the (low) counts. The NORTA correlation-matching the panel cites partially fixes this (it solves for the latent corr that yields a target rank corr), but it must be done JOINTLY with the chosen NB marginals and re-derived per category, not lifted from a continuous-data correlation matrix. CONSEQUENCE: the cross-category correlation layer must be calibrated and validated on the actual discrete totals (e.g. match Spearman/Kendall, not Pearson), or the simulated category co-movement will be biased — most for thin cats (SB, SV). Sources: 'A personal view on Sklar's theorem' (arXiv 2312.08570) https://arxiv.org/pdf/2312.08570 ('copulas lose their margin-free nature… Sklar's formalism does not allow neat separation between marginals and dependence anymore'); 'Modeling Count Data via Copulas' (arXiv 2012.02021) https://arxiv.org/pdf/2012.02021 ; Tau-informed initialization for discrete-margin copula MLE (arXiv 2511.13337) https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.13337 .

### Better-overall assessment
No FUNDAMENTALLY better overall ARCHITECTURE exists — my research confirms the synthesized design (tiered shared-projection-distribution layer → cheap analytic win-prob proxy → deep policy-aware paired-CRN season+playoff MC graded on Δ placement-utility with a shrinkage ladder and uncertainty band) is at or beyond every published method, each of which is a degraded special case (Rosenof's normal-only objective; RotoGraphs' roto-only sim; the GA fantasy-trade optimizer's static points objective; baseballforecaster's single-objective MC). It is correctly framed via Haugh-Singal (benchmark-relative expected payoff) and Bertsekas (policy-aware rollout). However, three FRAMING corrections (within the same architecture) are strictly better than what the panel converged on: (1) UNIFY the marginal+dependence model across BOTH tiers — the cheap proxy and the deep MC must use the SAME heteroscedastic NB/Skellam margins + shrunk discrete-copula correlation; the panel's two flagship recommendations are inconsistent (subject #4 says NB/Skellam, the competitive-frontier subject says adopt G-score's equal-variance Gaussian primitive). ### Biggest risks
RISK 1 — MODEL MISSPECIFICATION DOMINATES MC NOISE (the #1 risk). The whole edge rests on the per-category variance terms (CV/τ², and the discrete dispersion r) and the cross-category correlation matrix — and your existing src/optimizer/scenario_generator.py DEFAULT_CV/_RATE_STD are hand-set 'scaled to ~50%' guesses, not backtest-calibrated. If σ and ρ are wrong, no amount of CRN/control-variate/OCBA variance reduction helps — you precisely estimate a biased number. Demsyn-Jones documents exactly this overconfidence. MITIGATION: calibrate every per-category variance and the correlation matrix against the historical backtest (the design has the framework); validate by reliability/coverage on realized weekly category outcomes; report the band. RISK 2 — CHEAP-PROXY ↔ DEEP-MC INCONSISTENCY. The proxy ranks the wide candidate set and the deep MC only ever sees the finalists; if the proxy's distributional model (e.g. a Gaussian/G-score normal approx with P(tie)=0 and equal player variance) disagrees with the finalist sim (NB/Skellam, heteroscedastic, ties), good trades get filtered out before the deep tier can find them — a silent, uncorrectable error. MITIGATION: same margins/copula in both tiers; periodically deep-MC a random sample of proxy-rejected trades to measure rank agreement (Spearman) and recall. RISK 3 — UNMODELED AVAILABILITY/INJURY. The largest real ROS value swing is whether acquired players stay healthy/active; if availability is a single deterministic playing-time scalar, the engine will over-value injury-prone stars and mis-grade IL-stash/streaming trades, and there is no academic prior to lean on. MITIGATION: explicit per-player games/IP-remaining survival distribution sampled in the MC. RISK 4 — POLICY INCOMPLETENESS / FROZEN OPPONENTS. If the inner policy is LP-only over the current roster with frozen opponents (no streaming, no opponent re-optimization), it systematically over-credits the user's trade and under-values pitching-category and streaming-flexibility trades; this biases EVERY grade in the same direction (toward 'your trade helps'). MITIGATION: bounded weekly streaming/FA step in-loop + policy-aware opponents. RISK 5 — COMPUTE/LATENCY BLOWUP. Policy-aware re-solve-LP-every-week × 26 weeks + playoffs × ~1e3-5e3 CRN seasons × a candidate set, with a hierarchical parameter draw, is heavy for a web request; naive equal allocation across candidates wastes ~10× vs OCBA. MITIGATION: OCBA budget allocation + control-variate (proxy) + sequential stopping; async finalists only; cache the shared projection layer. RISK 6 — OVERFITTING THE SHRINKAGE-BLEND WEIGHT ON A NEAR-UNPOWERED 1-BIT CHAMP LABEL. Tuning the Δcat-wins→Δplayoff→Δchamp blend on a single ~1%-edge binary outcome risks selection bias; the design must use leakage-safe CPCV + Deflated-Sharpe/PBO deflation + a Minimum-Backtest-Length power check (subject #8) and shrink the blend weight toward equal-weight, or 'backtest-tuned' becomes 'backtest-overfit.' RISK 7 — DISCRETE-COPULA CALIBRATION ERROR (subject #4 trap): inducing dependence with a correlation matrix estimated as if data were continuous biases thin-category co-movement; calibrate the latent correlation jointly with the NB margins via rank-correlation matching. RISK 8 — SINGLE-SOURCE-OF-TRUTH COUPLING: making this engine power every surface means a miscalibration or a slow path propagates app-wide; gate behind the cheap tier for interactive surfaces and the deep tier async, with the Model-Confidence-Set / DM tests (subject #8) certifying any variant before it becomes canonical.
